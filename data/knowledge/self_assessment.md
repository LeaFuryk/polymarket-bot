# Self Assessment

## Known Biases to Avoid

1. **24h Anchoring Bias**: Do NOT let the daily BTC change dominate your reasoning. The 24h change has low predictive value for 5-min outcomes.

2. **Bearish Bias on Red Days**: Avoid betting bearish on every 5-min candle just because the daily candle is red. This loses money on the ~40% of candles that go up on bearish days.

3. **Low Confidence Trading**: Trades at 0.52-0.58 confidence are low quality. Only trade when confidence >= 0.6.

4. **Overtrading**: Not every cycle needs a trade. A well-timed HOLD is better than a low-edge trade that costs API fees.

5. **Ignoring Flat Markets**: When BTC is consolidating (Open = Close), "up" wins by default. Don't force directional calls in sideways action.

6. **Momentum Exhaustion Blindness**: Seeing 4+ consecutive candles in one direction and still betting continuation at high prices (>0.75) is a critical error. HOWEVER - Session 4 shows this needs nuance: in STRONG trends (6+ candles, >$100 move), continuation at 0.40-0.60 is actually the correct play.

7. **Premature Exit Syndrome**: Exiting quality positions too early due to fear. However, need better discrimination on when to hold vs exit.

8. **Mean Reversion Overconfidence**: The most dangerous bias. After learning the mean reversion pattern, becoming TOO confident in fade setups without checking broader market context. Mean reversion is probabilistic, not guaranteed.

9. **Ignoring Trend Context**: Trading mean reversion signals in isolation without checking if BTC is in a sustained 20-30 minute trend. When BTC drops $166+ over 30+ minutes, 5-minute exhaustion patterns are unreliable.

10. **Pattern Rigidity**: Repeating the same failed setup multiple times in a session. When a pattern fails twice consecutively, the market is telling you conditions have changed. Must adapt or HOLD.

11. **Failure to Recognize Regime Change**: Not detecting when market shifts from choppy (where mean reversion works) to trending (where it fails). Need to monitor 30-minute context continuously.

12. **NEW - Trend-Following Hesitation**: After Sessions 2-3 losses on mean reversion, may develop fear of trading in trending markets. Session 4 proves that trend-following WITH DISCIPLINE (entry <0.60, 6+ consecutive candles) is highly profitable. Don't let past mean reversion failures prevent correct trend-following trades.

Before ANY trade, must verify:

1. **[ ] 30-Minute Trend Check**:

2. **[ ] Strategy Selection**:

3. **[ ] Entry Price Validation**:
   - Mean reversion: must be <0.50 (ideally <0.45)

4. **[ ] Confidence Reality Check**:

5. **[ ] Time Remaining**:
   - <90 seconds: HOLD (no new entries)
   - 90-180 seconds: only if confidence >0.65 AND price excellent

6. **[ ] Recent Pattern Success**:
