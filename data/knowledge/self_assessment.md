# Self Assessment

## Known Biases to Avoid

1. **24h Anchoring Bias**: Do NOT let the daily BTC change dominate your reasoning. The 24h change has low predictive value for 5-min outcomes.

2. **Bearish Bias on Red Days**: Avoid betting bearish on every 5-min candle just because the daily candle is red. This loses money on the ~40% of candles that go up on bearish days.

3. **Low Confidence Trading**: Trades at 0.52-0.58 confidence are low quality. Only trade when confidence >= 0.6.

4. **Overtrading**: Not every cycle needs a trade. A well-timed HOLD is better than a low-edge trade that costs API fees.

5. **Ignoring Flat Markets**: When BTC is consolidating (Open = Close), "up" wins by default. Don't force directional calls in sideways action.

6. **Momentum Exhaustion Blindness**: Seeing 4+ consecutive candles in one direction and still betting continuation at high prices (>0.75) is a critical error. HOWEVER - in STRONG trends (6+ candles, >$100 move), continuation at 0.23-0.60 is actually the correct play.

7. **Premature Exit Syndrome**: Exiting quality positions too early due to fear. However, Sessions 7-8 show that profit-taking at 0.66-0.93 when bought at 0.23-0.51 is actually optimal strategy.

8. **Mean Reversion Overconfidence**: The most dangerous bias. After learning the mean reversion pattern, becoming TOO confident in fade setups without checking broader market context. Mean reversion is probabilistic, not guaranteed.

9. **Ignoring Trend Context**: Trading mean reversion signals in isolation without checking if BTC is in a sustained 20-30 minute trend. When BTC drops $166+ over 30+ minutes, 5-minute exhaustion patterns are unreliable.

10. **Pattern Rigidity**: Repeating the same failed setup multiple times in a session. When a pattern fails twice consecutively, the market is telling you conditions have changed. Must adapt or HOLD.

11. **Failure to Recognize Regime Change**: Not detecting when market shifts from choppy (where mean reversion works) to trending (where it fails). Need to monitor 30-minute context continuously.

12. **Trend-Following Hesitation**: After Sessions 2-3 losses on mean reversion, may develop fear of trading in trending markets. Sessions 4-8 prove that trend-following WITH DISCIPLINE (entry 0.23-0.60, 6+ consecutive candles) is highly profitable. Don't let past mean reversion failures prevent correct trend-following trades.

13. **Trend Continuation Overconfidence**: Even in strong trends, individual candles can reverse. After Session 4's 7-0 trend-following success, there's risk of assuming EVERY candle in a trend will continue. Reality: trends are probabilistic (65% win rate), not deterministic.

14. **Ignoring Micro-Reversals**: When a strong trend has 1 counter-trend candle, the next candle often (but not always) resumes the trend. This is a tradeable pattern but requires reduced confidence (0.58-0.62) and excellent entry pricing (<0.50).

15. **Momentum Chasing Bias**: Chasing continuation after large moves at expensive prices. After BTC moved +$186 (strong UP), buying UP at 0.66 and 0.81 both failed. This is the inverse of the original "momentum exhaustion blindness" - it's chasing the move AFTER it's already happened. Must recognize exhaustion risk after >$150 moves and avoid entries >0.60 in these conditions.

16. **Recency Bias After Winning Strategy**: After Session 4's 78% win rate, Sessions 5-6 showed 50% win rates. The strategy is still valid (overall 17W-9L = 65% across 5 sessions) but not infallible. Don't let one great session create overconfidence.

17. **Profit-Taking Hesitation**: Sessions 7-8 show that exiting profitable positions at 0.66-0.93 (when bought at 0.23-0.51) has 100% success rate (5/5). Don't hold through resolution hoping for slightly more profit when you can lock in gains. The edge is in the exit as much as the entry.

18. **Loss-Holding Stubbornness**: When a setup is clearly wrong and time allows (>120s), taking a small loss early can prevent larger loss at resolution. Don't stubbornly hold bad positions hoping for reversal.

19. **NEW - Greed After Profit**: Session 8 Cycle 11 showed perfect profit-taking (sold at 0.71, bought at 0.37 = +$20.40). Don't get greedy and hold for the last few cents when you're already up 20%+. Lock in the gain.

20. **NEW - Ignoring Small Consistent Moves**: Session 8 showed that even small moves ($11-$50) can continue in trending environments with 6+ consecutive candles. Don't dismiss micro-trends as "too small to matter."
