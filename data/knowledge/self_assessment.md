# Self Assessment

## Known Biases to Avoid

1. **24h Anchoring Bias**: Do NOT let the daily BTC change dominate reasoning. Low predictive value for 5-min outcomes.

2. **Bearish Bias on Red Days**: Avoid betting bearish on every candle just because the daily candle is red.

3. **Low Confidence Trading**: Trades at 0.52-0.58 confidence are low quality. Only trade when confidence >= 0.6.

4. **Overtrading**: Not every cycle needs a trade. A well-timed HOLD is better than a low-edge trade.

5. **Ignoring Flat Markets**: When BTC is consolidating, "up" wins by default. Don't force directional calls.

6. **Mean Reversion Overconfidence**: After learning mean reversion, becoming too confident without checking broader market context. Mean reversion is probabilistic, not guaranteed.

7. **Ignoring Trend Context**: Trading mean reversion in isolation without checking if BTC is in a sustained trend. In strong trends, exhaustion patterns are unreliable.

8. **Pattern Rigidity**: Repeating the same failed setup. When a pattern fails twice consecutively, conditions have changed. Must adapt or HOLD.

9. **Momentum Chasing**: Chasing continuation after large moves (>$150) at expensive prices (>0.60).

10. **Entry Discipline Violations**: NEVER enter at >0.65. This is the single most important rule.

## Confidence Calibration

**Mean Reversion in Choppy Markets**: 0.60-0.65
**Trend-Following in Strong Trends**: 0.58-0.64
**Profit-Taking Exits**: 0.68-0.70
**After Large Move (>$150)**: 0.45-0.56

## Critical Rules

- After 2 failed mean reversions in trend: STOP, switch strategy
- After 3 losses in session: reduce sizes by 30%
- STRICT <0.65 entry for all strategies (no exceptions)
- 30-minute trend check is MANDATORY before every trade
