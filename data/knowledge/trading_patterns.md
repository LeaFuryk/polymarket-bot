# Trading Patterns

## Key Principles for 5-Min BTC Candle Markets

1. **24h BTC change is NOT predictive** for 5-min candles. ~40% of 5-min candles go opposite to the daily trend. Do not anchor on the daily direction.

2. **Micro-momentum matters**: The last 3-6 five-minute candles are the best predictor of the next candle. Look for momentum continuation or exhaustion patterns.

3. **Mean reversion after 3+ same-direction candles**: After 3 or more consecutive candles in one direction, there is an increased probability of reversal. BUT this is context-dependent - see Trend Context Rules below.

4. **Spread dynamics**: When Up/Down mid prices diverge significantly from summing to $1.00, there may be a pricing inefficiency to exploit.

5. **Volume confirmation**: Higher volume on directional candles confirms momentum; low volume moves are less reliable.

6. **Flat candles are common**: BTC can remain at the same price across multiple 5-min candles. When BTC Open = BTC Close, the "up" outcome wins by default. This creates opportunities when "up" is underpriced during sideways action.

## Strategy Selection by Market Regime

### Choppy/Range-Bound (BTC range <$50 over 30 min):
- PRIMARY: HOLD unless exceptional setup
- Mean reversion only at <0.28 entry (excellent pricing)
- Look for underpriced "up" in flat markets
- **CRITICAL**: Small moves ($10-30) are coin flips - avoid trading
- Reduced position sizing (40-50 shares) if trading

### Trending (6+ consecutive candles, >$100 move):
- PRIMARY: Trend-following at good prices (0.22-0.45)
- AVOID: Mean reversion (high failure rate in these conditions)
- Profit-taking: exit at 0.60-0.75 when bought at 0.22-0.45
- Standard position sizing (50-60 shares)

### Exhaustion (>$150 move in 2-3 candles):
- AVOID: Chasing continuation at >0.45
- PREFERRED: HOLD and wait for consolidation
- Counter-trend only if entry <0.30 and strong exhaustion signals

## Entry Pricing Discipline (STRICT - UPDATED)

*Mean Reversion (fade trades)*:
- Excellent: <0.25 (size up to 60 shares)
- Good: 0.25-0.30 (standard 50-55 shares)
- Marginal: 0.30-0.37 (reduce to 40-45 shares)
- Avoid: 0.37-0.45 (insufficient edge)
- **NEVER**: >0.45 (catastrophic risk/reward)

*Trend-Following (continuation trades)*:
- Excellent: 0.22-0.35 (size up to 60 shares)
- Good: 0.35-0.45 (standard 50-55 shares)
- Marginal: 0.45-0.50 (reduce to 40-45 shares)
- **NEVER**: >0.50 (prevents catastrophic losses)

## Time Management Rules

- **No new entries with <90s remaining**
- **Exit window 150-180s**: If losing >25%, exit to preserve capital
- **Profit-taking window 120-180s**: If winning >25%, EXIT immediately
- **<60s**: HOLD all positions unless catastrophic loss developing
- **<20s**: MANDATORY HOLD - no trades

## Pre-Trade Checklist (MANDATORY)

1. **30-Minute Trend Check**: Calculate BTC move over last 6-8 candles
2. **Regime Identification**: Choppy (<$50 range) vs Trending (>$100 move) vs Exhaustion (>$150 spike)
3. **Entry Price Validation**: Must meet STRICT pricing thresholds or automatic HOLD
4. **Time Management**: No new entries with <90s remaining
5. **Recent Pattern Check**: Has this exact setup failed in last 3 cycles? If yes, HOLD
6. **Choppy Market Filter**: If range-bound with no clear momentum, default to HOLD

## Key Failure Modes to Avoid

1. Trading in choppy/sideways markets without exceptional setup
2. Buying at >0.50 for trend-following or >0.45 for mean reversion
3. Holding losing positions past 180s mark when down >25%
4. Not taking profits when up >25% with 120-180s remaining
5. Trading small moves (<$50) at high confidence
6. Using mean reversion in trending markets
7. Repeating the same failed setup without adaptation
8. Chasing continuation after large move (>$150) at expensive prices
9. Over-relying on indicators without price action confirmation
10. Panic selling at extreme losses with <60s remaining
11. Buying mean reversion at >0.37 - insufficient edge
12. **NEW**: Waiting for extreme exit prices (0.10-0.20) - take profits at 0.25-0.35
13. **NEW**: Entering trend-following at >0.50 - risk/reward is poor
